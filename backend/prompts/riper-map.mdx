# /riper-map

This is the Innovate-phase prompt. It generates the alternatives table that feeds Plan. You diverge here so the Plan phase has real options to choose between, not a default. For the full protocol context, see [RIPER](/backend/workflow/riper).

## When to use

- After [/riper-goals](/backend/prompts/riper-goals) has produced clear goals and falsifiable success criteria.
- During RIPER's Innovate phase, before committing to a single approach.
- When a single approach feels obvious — that's exactly when you most need divergent options to confirm or refute it.

## The prompt

```
You are Opus running Stage 1 RIPER, Innovate phase. Mode: thinking only.
No code in this turn.

Below this prompt I will paste:
1. The goals and success criteria block from /riper-goals.
2. Any constraint context (existing modules, prior ADRs, wave assessment,
   non-functional limits).

Your job: produce at least three viable approaches that could plausibly meet
the goals. Three is the floor. Five is the ceiling. Each approach must be
meaningfully different from the others.

For each approach, output exactly these fields:

### Name
A short label, two to four words. Use it as the heading.

### Description
One paragraph in plain language. Describe the approach, not the code. A
reader who has never seen this codebase should understand what the approach
is and what shape it takes.

### Trade-offs
Three to five bullet points. Each bullet is balanced: it must mention both a
strength and a cost. Bad: "fast". Good: "fastest read path of the three,
at the cost of a heavier write path and a second consistency boundary".

### Wave fit
One sentence. Does this approach match the project's current wave, or does
it assume a higher or lower one? If it assumes a higher wave, say so plainly.

Hard constraints:
- Do not recommend an approach. Do not say "I would choose X". Do not rank
  them. Do not pre-sort them by your preference. Innovate is divergent
  thinking; ranking happens in the Plan phase.
- The approaches must be meaningfully different. Different architectural
  shape. Different trade-off profile. Not three flavors of the same idea
  with renamed variables.
- If the constraints genuinely admit only one viable shape, report that as
  "Only one viable approach" with the reason and the constraints that
  forced it. That is a real finding, not a failure.

STOP after the alternatives. Do not pick. Wait for confirmation before
moving to Plan.
```

## What you get back

Three or more named approaches. Each has a description, a balanced trade-off list, and a wave-fit sentence.

A few things to remember about this output:

- **It is input to Plan.** It is not a decision yet. The Plan phase is where you pick.
- **The approaches should feel meaningfully different.** Different architectural shape, different trade-off profile. If they read like three names for the same idea, the Innovate phase did its job poorly.
- **"Only one viable approach" is a valid output.** It means the constraints genuinely narrow the design space. Document the constraints that forced it, then move to Plan.

<Tip>
If the approaches all read like minor variations on the same idea, re-run /riper-map and ask for genuinely divergent shapes. Force divergence by naming a constraint to invert ("now generate one approach that uses a queue instead of synchronous calls").
</Tip>

## What to do next

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read all approaches with their trade-offs">
    Read top to bottom. Resist the pull to pick a favorite on the first pass. The trade-offs are what you are evaluating, not the names.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the approaches are meaningfully different">
    Different architectural shape, not three names for the same approach. If two approaches collapse into the same trade-off profile when you squint, they are not real alternatives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-run if approaches feel too similar or one dominates trivially">
    Ask for stronger divergence or a deeper trade-off table. A trivial winner often means the trade-offs were under-specified, not that the choice is obvious.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move to RIPER's Plan phase">
    When you have real alternatives, move to Plan to pick one. The pick lands in the [module manifest](/backend/workflow/module-manifest) along with the rationale, often as an [ADR](/glossary).
  </Step>
</Steps>
